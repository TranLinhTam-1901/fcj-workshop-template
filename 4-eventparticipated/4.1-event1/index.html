Event 1

Summary Report: “AWS FIRST CLOUD AI JOURNEY - COMMUNITY DAY”

Event Objectives

  • Networking and Inspiration: This was not merely a seminar for listening to presentations. Its primary objective was to create an environment where members of the technology community could actively network, initiate conversations, and inspire one another. The event encouraged participants to seek potential collaborators and build new professional relationships within the industry.
  • Career Orientation in the AI Era: The event provided participants with practical and realistic insights into the future of the job market as AI continues to evolve rapidly. It aimed to help engineers and students understand the changing industry landscape, where demand is shifting from simply writing code toward roles such as Platform Engineering and operating large-scale, complex systems.
  • Equipping Participants with Global Competitiveness: The speakers emphasized that Vietnamese professionals should not limit themselves to the domestic market but instead strengthen their capabilities to compete internationally. To achieve this, they highlighted the importance of building a solid technical foundation, understanding real-world business use cases, and delivering production-ready products rather than stopping at proof-of-concept (PoC) demos.
  • Sharing Practical Knowledge and Real-world Experience: The event provided valuable perspectives from professionals directly involved in Operations (Ops) and Systems Engineering. Participants gained practical lessons on infrastructure, operational management, and how to apply technology securely and reliably in enterprise environments, particularly within highly regulated industries such as Banking and Finance.

Speakers

  • Anh Pham - Cloud Consultant, G-AsiaPacific Vietnam
  • Tinh Truong - Platform Engineer, Gothamic
  • Thinh Nguyen - Devops Engineer, FCAJ
  • UTM Team - Participating in a 36-hour Hackathon
  • Duc Dao - Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics
  • Vy Lam - Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank

Key Highlights

The Current State of the Software Industry and the Jevons Paradox in the AI Era

  • Explosive Growth in Software Demand: The speaker illustrated this concept using the LED light analogy. As AI makes software development more affordable and efficient, the demand for software will not decrease; instead, it will grow exponentially.
  • The Rise of “Non-IT” Professionals: A new wave of individuals without traditional technology backgrounds—such as lawyers and doctors—are achieving top rankings in Hackathon competitions by effectively leveraging AI.
  • Challenges During the Transition Period: As major technology companies continue investing heavily in AI infrastructure while optimizing costs in other business areas, the short-term job market has experienced a slowdown, making it more challenging for software professionals.

The Shift in Engineering Roles: Platform Engineering & Software Fixers

  • Software Re-engineering (Fixers): A new category of engineering roles is emerging, focusing on debugging, optimizing, and improving AI-generated source code and large volumes of Minimum Viable Products (MVPs).
  • Platform Engineering: Demand for Platform Engineers continues to increase as organizations require professionals capable of building, operating, and maintaining large-scale software platforms in the AI era.

Hiring Standards in the GenAI Era: Backend Fundamentals Remain the Foundation

  • Core Technical Competencies: Based on real hiring practices in the banking industry, enterprises continue to prioritize engineers with strong backend fundamentals, including system architecture, data security, password hashing and encryption, and database administration. - GenAI Is a Significant Advantage, Not Everything: Practical experience with GenAI has become an important competitive advantage—and increasingly an expected competency—but it cannot replace solid software engineering fundamentals.

AI Agent Governance & Model Context Protocol (MCP)

  • Agent Authorization: The speakers introduced methods for designing layered configurations that clearly define what AI Agents are permitted to do, who is authorized to perform specific operations, and the level of autonomy granted when acting on behalf of users.
  • MCP Security: They emphasized the importance of understanding how to secure Model Context Protocol (MCP) architectures when deploying autonomous AI systems.

Secure Infrastructure Management with Infrastructure as Code (IaC) Using Terraform

  • Understanding Terraform Correctly: The speakers corrected the common misconception that “Terraform is a virtual machine.” In reality, Terraform is an Infrastructure as Code (IaC) tool that provisions and manages infrastructure through code rather than manual click-based operations.
  • Practical Benefits: Infrastructure changes become version-controlled through Git commits, creating a complete audit trail while making it easy to reproduce identical infrastructure configurations across different regions (for example, from US-East-2 to US-East-1).

Key Takeaways

Design Mindset

  • Building Systems, Not Just Applications: One of the most valuable lessons can be summarized by the statement: “Building a system is not just about making it work. It must operate securely, reliably, and ultimately serve its users.”
  • The Importance of Context: AI cannot inherently understand the unique business challenges of a company or the specific requirements of an individual project. Humans must provide accurate context, domain knowledge, and well-defined instructions for AI to produce meaningful results.

System Architecture & Operations

  • Managing Infrastructure Through Code: I gained a deeper understanding of how modern infrastructure is managed using Terraform to automate deployments while improving reliability, compliance, and auditability.
  • Secure AI Governance: I recognized the importance of defining clear operational boundaries and authorization levels for AI Agents to ensure security and protect sensitive data.

Applying to Work

  • Strengthen Backend Fundamentals: Continue investing in core software engineering competencies—including security, database optimization, and system design—while learning how to effectively integrate AI into development workflows.
  • Adopt Terraform for Infrastructure Management: Replace manual infrastructure configuration with Infrastructure as Code using Terraform to improve version control, maintainability, and environment reproducibility.
  • Apply GenAI with Domain-specific Context: Improve prompting techniques and AI-powered applications by enriching them with business context, domain knowledge, and real-world business rules to generate more accurate and reliable results.
  • Build Systems with Security as a Priority: Always prioritize security, compliance, and auditability when designing systems, especially those that incorporate AI technologies.

Event Experience

Participating in the FCAJ Community Day was an extremely valuable and practical experience, providing diverse perspectives on today’s technology landscape:

A Vibrant Community Atmosphere and Meaningful Networking

  • The event attracted an exceptionally large number of participants, with the crowd extending up to the 36th floor. This clearly demonstrated the spirit of networking and mutual inspiration, rather than simply attending a traditional seminar.

Learning from Real-world Industry Perspectives

  • I had the opportunity to listen to insightful macro-level analyses from Nguyễn Gia Hưng (Solutions Architect at AWS Vietnam and Founder of FCAJ), whose presentations helped clarify many common misconceptions among software engineers during this transitional AI era.
  • The sessions were designed to provide broad perspectives rather than focusing too deeply on a single technology, making the content relevant and valuable for professionals from various backgrounds, including Developers, Testers, DevOps Engineers, and UI/UX Designers.

Understanding Your True Value in the Job Market

  • The event encouraged participants to remain rational amid the rapid rise of AI by reinforcing the importance of returning to strong engineering fundamentals and adopting a sustainable, system-oriented mindset instead of blindly chasing emerging trends.

Networking and discussions

  • The “FCAJ Community” Spirit: The workshop successfully fulfilled its goal of becoming more than just a technical seminar. It created an open environment that encouraged participants to actively initiate conversations, expand their professional networks, and discover potential teammates or future collaborators.
  • Cross-functional Knowledge Sharing: The event brought together professionals from various disciplines—including Development, Testing, Backend Engineering, DevOps, and UI/UX—to openly discuss how AI is reshaping the technology job market. These discussions helped bridge different perspectives and foster a better understanding of real-world engineering and operational challenges.

Lessons learned

  • Embrace Industry Transformation Instead of Fearing AI: One of the biggest lessons was understanding the paradox that as AI reduces the cost of software development, the amount of software that needs to be maintained and operated will grow exponentially. Rather than worrying about AI, engineers should proactively prepare for emerging opportunities in Platform Engineering and Software Re-engineering, where maintaining, optimizing, and operating AI-generated software will become increasingly important.
  • Backend Fundamentals Remain the Anchor: Even in highly demanding enterprise environments such as the banking industry, GenAI is considered a valuable competitive advantage rather than a replacement for core engineering capabilities such as system architecture, data encryption, security, and database management.
  • Moving Beyond “Click Engineering”: Modern infrastructure management should rely on Infrastructure as Code (Terraform) to maintain infrastructure changes through Git version control, ensuring security, compliance, auditability, and consistent environment replication.
  • The Highest Standard for System Design: The most important architectural principle reinforced throughout the event is that a successful system is not merely one that functions. It must operate securely, reliably, and ultimately deliver meaningful value to its users.

Overall, the event not only decoded the practical shifts within the software market in the face of the GenAI wave, but also helped me redefine my self-worth. The combination of a technology-mastery mindset (Platform Engineering, IaC with Terraform) and returning to solidify core technical capabilities (Backend Core) serves as a steadfast anchor to breakthrough in this new era. The workshop deeply instilled in me a fundamental truth: a modern software system is not merely about being “functional”; its ultimate destination is to operate securely, reliably, and entirely in service of delivering the best user experience.
